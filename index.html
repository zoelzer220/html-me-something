Parenting Toddlers 101

Kids are nuts. Raising them is enjoyable and rewarding, however there will be moments they drive you absolutely crazy. These 7 reminders will help you rock the parenting game and hopefully make the moments you want to rip your hair out a little easier.

1. Have patience.

Kids can sense when you're about to lose it, inturn they feed off your energy. They also seem to strike when you are weak. Take a deep breath, you can do this! When they are out of earshot you can scream or express any frustration, until then hold it together.

2. Put yourself in their shoes.

Each day kids are seeing, hearing or experincing something new. They are trying to figure out how the world works, while at the same time learning the expectations you have of them. Life can seem overwhelming & complicated, similar to how we feel sometimes. Cut them some slack.

3. Let them teach you.

Understand that they have just as much to teach you as you have to teach them. Let their sense of wonder inspire you. Appreciate the little things. Get excited about sharing some fruit snacks.

4. Remember they get hangry just like we do.

If they are acting like a mad(wo)man -- they are probably hungry. If its close to mealtime or they haven't ate in awhile they are probably hungry. If they see a snack they want or get reminded of food they will probably be hungry.

5. Take things one step at a time.

Things seem so much easier when you look at them in smaller pieces. If you focus on one step they are more likely to listen.

6. Acknowledge your emotions.

It's okay to be sad or mad or even angry. You're entitled to you own feelings, just like we teach our children. However, the part that matters is realizing them & handling them in a constructive manner. It's amazing what a deep breath can do.

7. Have fun!

Don't take life too seriously. Take time to enjoy it, have fun with your babes. Do a funny dance. Play dress up. Sing at the top of your lungs.

By any means, this list is NOT exhaustive but it can put you on track to earning a parent of the year award, even if it's just in the eyes of your own children.

BEST OF LUCK! YOU GOT THIS.
